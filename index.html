About me

I am a landscape ecologist and conservation biologist. While I started assessing the impact of landscape structure on primates, I have expanded my research to encompass the study of other biological groups, including vertebrates, invertebrates and plants. Currently, my aim is to comprehend the ways in which populations and communities responses to changes in natural environments caused by human activities. By exploring questions at global, regional, landscape, and local scales, my ultimate goal is to make valuable contributions to the preservation of biodiversity.
